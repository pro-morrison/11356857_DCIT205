GET TO KNOW US

Welcome to the dynamic and forward-thinking Computer Science department at the University of Ghana. Our Ph.D. program is meticulously crafted to offer scholars an immersive and advanced education in computer science. We foster groundbreaking research and provide an enriching environment for individuals dedicated to pushing the boundaries of knowledge in the ever-evolving landscape of technology.

ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS

Welcome to the Ph.D. program at the Computer Science department of the University of Ghana. To ensure a smooth application process, please meet the following key admission requirements:

  • Educational Qualifications: Possession of a master's degree in a related field .
  • Research Proposal: Submit a well-defined research proposal outlining your intended area of study and research objectives.
  • Letters of Recommendation: Provide three letters of recommendation from academic referees who can attest to your research capabilities.

We look forward to receiving applications from motivated individuals who meet these essential requirements. For more details or inquiries, please don't hesitate to contact us.

PROGRAM OVERVIEW

Our doctoral programme is a full-time research programme with expected duration of 4-years.
For the successful completion of this programme, a high level of commitment is required.
Students are therefore expected to reside close to the University and be a full-time participant in the program.
With guidance from postgraduate committee, Ph.D. students will create a personalized program of study that includes a major area of specialization and at least two minor areas of specialization.
In addition to coursework, the department holds regular seminars where students and faculty review and critique research in the field. To foster prudent critique, students are expected to join at most one research lab and participate in research activities carried out by the group. The research areas of the department are Intelligent Space and Machine Learning, Data Science/Big Data Analytics, Networking and Communications Engineering, and Cyber Security
