GET TO KNOW US

Welcome to the vibrant and innovative Computer Science department at the University of Ghana. Our postgraduate programs (MSc and MPhil) are designed to offer an enriching and advanced education in computer science, preparing students for impactful careers in the ever-evolving landscape of technology.

ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS

We seek to admit highly motivated individuals with a passion for advancing their knowledge in computing. Kindly review the following admission requirements and procedures to facilitate a seamless application process:

For MSc Program:

  • A relevant bachelor's degree in Computer Science or a related field.
  • A minimum of 2.0 gpa from accredited universities
  • Resume/CV detailing relevant experience.
  • TOEFL/IELTS scores for non-native English speakers.

For MPhil Program:

  • A relevant master's degree in Computer Science or a related field.
  • Research proposal outlining your intended research topic.
  • Resume/CV detailing relevant experience.
  • TOEFL/IELTS scores for non-native English speakers.

We are excited about the prospect of welcoming dedicated students to our dynamic academic community.

PROGRAM OVERVIEW

The Department of Computer Science – University of Ghana currently runs Masters of Science (MSc.) in Computer Science and Master of Philosophy (MPhil.) in Computer Science. The MSc. is a 1-year (weekend/evening) programme with a capstone project while the MPhil is a 2-year (full-time) programme.
The first year of the MPhil is taught course and second year is research and dissertation. Both programmes are accelerated master’s programme specially designed to train current and future computer scientist who are available for full-time or part-time study.
Students enrolled in these programmes are equipped with broader knowledge in theoretical computer science and advance technical skills to approach science in technology as a disruptive force to manage complex problems of the global society.
At the end of these programmes, students will gain hands-on experience in solving complex, and unstructured scientific problems while learning subjects like Advance Databases, Advance Data Structures and Algorithms and Networks.
